Diving in Diani

Kenya has world-class dive sites all along its fabulous coastline, there are several dive schools in Diani Beach: Diving the Crab at Nomads, Diani Marine and Ocean Tribe, and all of them will take experienced divers out beyond the reef to dive with whale shark, dolphins, turtles, rays and even humpback whale depending on the time of year. Inexperienced divers can choose from taster sessions or a range of formal PADI qualifications. Many dive shops close during the rainy season which is April to June. November to February is the season for whales and whale sharks in Diani.

Diani Ride a Camel

A trip to Diani isn’t complete without a camel ride along the beach. The kids love it and I’ve seen many terrified adults cling on as the camel lowers them precariously toward the ground. Pay 500 bob / 5 USD for 15 minutes – you’ll find the camel guy all along the beach as he walks the entire length throughout the day.

Diani Go Kitesurfing

When the wind picks up, you will see kite surfers all along the Diani Beach coastline. With shallow waters and consistent winds, Diani is one of the best places to kitesurf and learn to kitesurf in Kenya. Kenya is famed for its ancient shipping routes and relies heavily on the two winds, the Kusi which blows up from the south from April to September and the Kaskazi which blows over from India from December to April. Hop over to H20 Extreme at Kenya airways or Quest Kitesurfing at Diani Lodge.

Diani Go Horse-riding

Diani beach Climate

The climate in Diani Beach is tropical, wet and warm. The best travel time is from late November to mid-March. After that it gets uncomfortably hot with increased humidity. The worst travel time is in the main rainy season from April to June. It usually rains only briefly and then the sun shines again, but it is also possible to suffer a continuous rain for days. Also in the 2nd rainy season, October-November, you have to reckon with inconveniences. As it is never cold on rainy days you can still sit at the pool bars or even take beach walks. Aside from the several bars and the shopping malls in Ukunda, there are no other attractions that you can visit in the rain; you can only tour Mombasa, visiting the old town, Fort Jesus, some temples, shops and shopping malls. Rainy days can be a bit boring in Diani.

How To Get to Diani Beach

There are 5+ flights daily from Nairobi to Ukunda Airstrip, which is in central Diani. Flights from Nairobi depart from Jomo Kenyatta International airport or Wilson, which is a smaller, easier airport to traverse. Return flights start from 5000 KSH or 50 USD. From Mombasa, take a taxi to Diani Beach. It takes about 90 minutes and costs approximately 5000 KSH / 50 USD. The SGR train – goes from Nairobi to Mombasa in 6 hours. It’s fast, clean and comfortable and a fraction of the price, but you will still need to pay for the taxi from Diani to Mombasa. You can drive to Diani from Nairobi. It takes about 9 hours. My advice is to head through Shimba Hills, rather than through Mombasa. It doesn’t add any time on and it is much quieter and more scenic the closest airport to us is Malindi Airport, just 45 minutes’ drive away, with frequent daily flights from Nairobi.

Ukunda Airstrip? Can I also fly into Mombasa?

Ukunda Airstrip is about 12 minutes away and easily accessible. We strongly recommend our guests to fly into Ukunda (as opposed to Mombasa). Ukunda is well served by Jambo jet and Fly540 (from JKIA) and by Safari link and Air Kenya (from Wilson). Moi international at Mombasa also offers many local and international services. When arriving to Mombasa please be aware there is a longer transfer as there is a crossing via the Likoni Ferry.
